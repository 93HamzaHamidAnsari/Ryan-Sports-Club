Recreational Sports Mission

Children's Instructional Gymnastics Program

The Department of Recreational Sports enhances the quality of life for the community by educating and encouraging participation in activities that promote healthy lifestyles, social interactions, and leadership skills. The department creates an atmosphere that encourages individuals to develop life-long involvement in recreational activities and is dedicated to meeting the changing needs of a diverse community by offering quality structured and informal recreational opportunities.

Whether you want to learn how to tumble, get some exercise or just have fun, you will find something that meets your gymnastic needs here. No previous experience necessary. We have friendly experienced instructors who will provide children with guided instruction on various events to improve comfort, flexibility, and strength while giving positive reinforcement. We pride ourselves on providing a quality program that is fun and safe. Each class will be led by two enthusiastic instructors to ensure everyone participates and receives positive corrective feedback. On the first day of class, there will be a parent/guardian orientation that we encourage all parents to attend.

Swimming

Learn to swim regardless of your age! We offer swim lessons for infants starting at 6 months of age all the way to adults.

The sport of Swimming for athletes with an impairment is governed by the IPC, and co-ordinated by the IPC Swimming Technical Committee, using the International Swimming Federation’s (FINA) modified rules.
